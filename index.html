07 / 23 / 18

When rape is reported and nothing happens

This first part in the "Denied Justice" occasional series is an incredible piece of investigative journalism, and the result of years of reporting and data requests from Brandon Stahl, Jennifer Bjorhus and MaryJo Webster. After analyzing over 1,000 cases they found police were consistently failing to do basic policework in rape cases.

This is a problem that affected many survivors, so I didn't want to use only one image as the entry way to the story. Instead we went with a type treatment that highlighted how these women felt and the common thread through their stories. This project also encorporated new elements for the Star Tribune, such as the email sign-up and alert system and the option to subscribe to the paper from the article page.

HTML, CSS/SCSS, Illustrator, ai2html, Photoshop

07 / 23 / 18

‘I was just lost in the dark’

When the first part of the "Denied Justice" occasional series published we also put out this video interactive collecting the experiences of survivors reporting their assaults to the police — in their own words. Origianlly published with 12 women, it is now up to 16 survivors' stories and will continue to grow.

I went into this project with the sole purpose of showcasing the incredible photography and videography by Renee Jones Schneider and giving the importantce to these women's stories that they deserved. Keeping the media items as big as possible gave them the gravatis they deserved, and the video player design is quiet as to not distract from these stories.

HTML, CSS/SCSS, Illustrator, ai2html, Photoshop

04 / 29 / 18

Unstoppable and deadly, meth floods the state

This graphic accompanied an enterprise story from Stephen Montemayor about the flood of meth into Minnesota. Meth overdoses in Minnesota have killed more than 416 people in the past year. The Twin Cities have emerged as a distribution hub for the upper midwest.

I wanted to distill the information into easy-to-digest paragraphs and centered around the map. It helps illustrate the entire journey of the meth into and out of Minnesota and the busts with ties to the state. The use of one base map helps keep the information from getting overwhelming. I also created a gif to share with the stories.

HTML, CSS/SCSS, Illustrator, ai2html, Photoshop

03 / 02 / 18

Your guide to the 2018 Oscar nominations

This project was a fun chance to break out my dormant illustration skills and play around with color. For the main image, I wanted to create a cohesive illustration that represented the best picture nominees. Since the movies had such varying tones, I decided to equalize them by making them all black and white, and adding some yellow as a good pop.

For the presentation, I added a secondary navigation system so readers could skip to the categories they were interested in reading about, and carried some of the yellow throughout the project to give it a consistent feel. I also made a gif to share with the story.

HTML, CSS/SCSS, JS (jQuery, Scrollmagic), Photoshop

02 / 18 / 18

Will November bring a 'wave'?

Will republicans lose their majority after the midterm elections in November? I worked on this project (doing graphics and site development) with Judy Keen, who wrote a great story using historical data and current trends to address this question.

There were a few versions of these graphics we went through during a quick — one week — turnaround period for this piece. They were distilled down and became extremely simple, which helped readability and comprehension. I also made a gif to share with the story.

HTML, CSS/SCSS, Illustrator, ai2html, Photoshop

01 / 26 / 18

Essential Twin Cities Guide

Minneapolis was the sports hub of the country for a few weeks before the Super Bowl, and this guide was born to help show people all the things there are to do in the Twin Cities — besides just the big game.

This project was a huge undertaking and an unprecedented effort for me in UI/UX design thinking, the importance of prototyping, and the ability to adjust on the go. In the end, it succeeded in reaching hundreds of thousands of people in twon for the Super Bowl, and the functionality exceeded expectations. There was a promotional video and gif to accompany social media sharing.

HTML, CSS/SCSS, Sketch, Balsamiq, InVision
