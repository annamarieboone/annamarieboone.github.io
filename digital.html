02 / 18 / 18

Will November bring a 'wave'?

Will republicans lose their majority after the midterm elections in November? I worked on this project doing graphics and site development with Judy Keen, who wrote a great story using historical data to address this question.

There were a few versions of these graphics we went through during a fairly quick — one week — turnaround period for this piece. They were distilled down and became extremely simple, which helped readability and comprehension. I also made a gif to share with the story.

HTML, CSS/SCSS, Illustrator, ai2html, Photoshop

01 / 26 / 18

Essential Twin Cities Guide

Minneapolis was the sports hub of the country for a few weeks before the Super Bowl, and this guide was born to help show people all the things there are to do in the Twin Cities — besides the game.

This project was a huge undertaking, and one I will write more in-depth about in the future. The guide was an unprecedented effort for me in UI/UX design thinking, the importance of prototyping, and the ability to adjust on the go. In the end, it got over 305k views, and the functionality exceeded expectations. There was a promotional video and gif to accompany social media sharing.

HTML, CSS/SCSS, Sketch, Balsamiq, InVision

02 / 02 / 18

From $12 to $2,500 and counting

The Super Bowl coming to Minneapolis gave me a chance to work with the sports department and chart the prices of Super Bowl tickets. Turns out I could have afforded to go when it first started, but inflation pretty quickly stopped my chances of seeing it live.

This was my first project with a solo byline and one I was really excited about. There was a gif used to help promote sharing.

HTML, CSS/SCSS, JS (Scrollmagic, jQuery)

Pudge, the first pro

The very first professional football player was from Minnesota — and he was born only 10 blocks from where Super Bowl LII was played.

01 / 27 / 18

The state of Bud

Bud Grant, a 90-year-old former coach for the Vikings, once wore a short sleeve polo shirt in subzero weather. Just an important fact to get out there.

This project was my first time using CSS transitions and animations in the intro art of a story. This profile was a perspective of Bud many don't see, so cycling through who he is as people expect and then the fullscreen, color photo of him in his basement helped to set the (unexpected) scene. There was also a twitter card to share.

HTML, CSS/SCSS, JS (Scrollmagic, jQuery), Owl Carousel

11 / 17 / 17

A new Nicollet Mall

Nicollet Mall, a primary road in downtown Minneapolis got a new remake that emphasized pedestrian traffic.

This was a full page graphic that ran in the paper I reworked for web. The verical map was perfect for mobile, and on desktop I revealed certain pieces of inforamtion at different map points so they didn't overwhelm readers — or have the map be too small to read.

HTML, CSS/SCSS, JS (jQuery, Scrollmagic), ai2html

10 / 17 / 17

Take a Minnesota bike laws quiz

Minneapolis is a big city for bikers. In fact, a few of my collegues bike to work every day, even in the winter. While I'm not that dedicated yet, I will be getting a bike when it warms back up.

My first time illustrating a main story — I created vector art for each question in the bike laws quiz, 13 illustrations in total. I also animated a gif of a bike to use for social media sharing.

09 / 10 / 18

Road to Minneapolis

Leading up to Super Bowl LII, sports writer Mark Craig picked the two teams he thought would play in the championship every week. This was a template I designed that was used each week to help give these stories a different feel than an average sports story.

To help aid in the feel of a continued series, there is naviagtion at the top of the page and after his reasoning for his predictions. The photo treatment each week was done primarily by me, and helped to establish the real sporty nature of the sports.

HTML, CSS/SCSS, Photoshop

08 / 04 / 17

Renovation of the Minnesota State Captiol

For three years, the Minnesota State Capitol went under a massive, $310 million renovation.

This graphic had a lot of information on specific areas, but not many sections over all. I used the fullPage.js library to create sections for each area, and kept interactive pieces throughout (my favorite is sliding to see the difference after the artwork was restored).

HTML, CSS/SCSS, JS (jQuery, fullPage), Photoshop

12 / 07 / 15

The unsettled

The most important story I was a part of in college is this, a profile of a family forced to flee Syria who now live in Indiana. Writer Alden Woods, photographer Ike Hajinazarian and I reported this over the course of a semester, during which then-Governor and current Vice President Pence said he would not allow Syrian refugees in the country. This story won story of the year by the Associated Collegiate Press.

HTML, CSS/SCSS, Photoshop, ai2html
